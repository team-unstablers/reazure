# Privacy Policy

**re;azure** — a streaming-only Mastodon client for iOS / iPadOS / macOS

Effective date: July 11, 2026

> **TL;DR — re;azure does not collect any personal information. Your data belongs entirely to you.**

*[한국어 안내는 아래에 있습니다.](#개인정보-처리방침)*

## Overview

re;azure is a client application for Mastodon and other Mastodon-compatible (ActivityPub) servers, developed by team unstablers Inc.

We do **not** collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal information. The app contains no analytics, no advertising, no tracking, and no crash-reporting services. We do not operate any intermediary servers — nothing you do in the app ever passes through us.

## Data stored on your device

To function as a client, re;azure keeps a small amount of data **locally on your device only**:

- **Account information** — the address of the server(s) you sign in to and the OAuth access token issued by that server.
- **App preferences** — your settings (theme, notification sounds, etc.).
- **Media cache** — images may be cached temporarily on-device for performance.

This data never leaves your device, except when the app uses your access token to communicate with the server you signed in to. Deleting the app removes all of it.

## Network communication

re;azure communicates **directly and exclusively** with the Mastodon-compatible server(s) you choose to sign in to, using their public REST and streaming (WebSocket) APIs.

The content you post, read, favourite, or boost is processed and stored by **your server**, under that server's own privacy policy. We encourage you to review the privacy policy of the server you use, as it — not this document — governs how your data is handled there.

## Third parties

re;azure does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs. No data is shared with third parties.

## Children's privacy

re;azure does not collect personal information from anyone, including children.

## Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be published at this location with a revised effective date. Given the app's design (no data collection), we do not expect meaningful changes.
